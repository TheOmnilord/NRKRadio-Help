NRK Radio

Help & setup guide · updated July 2026

In short: Ask an Echo to play any NRK radio channel live, or to play the most recent NRK news bulletin. Say “Alexa, open Norwegian Radio” for NRK P1 Stor-Oslo, “…to play P2” for another channel, or “…for latest news” for the newest bulletin that has finished airing.

This is a personal, unpublished skill — a private project running only on its developer’s own Echo devices. It is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or connected to NRK, and it is not available to install. NRK’s livestreams are made available for private use; this page documents a private setup, not a service.

What it does

Two things. It plays NRK’s live radio channels — all 28 of them, including the district P1 variants — straight from NRK’s own streams. And it plays the latest news bulletin: you ask, and it finds the most recent NRK Nyheter that has actually finished broadcasting and plays that.

Nothing is downloaded or stored. The Echo streams directly from NRK, the same way the NRK Radio app does.

First-time setup

  1. Enable the skill on the Amazon account your Echo devices are signed in to. Because it is unpublished, it appears in the Alexa app under Your Skills with a Dev badge.
  2. Check the device language. The skill answers in English (UK) and English (US). Alexa has no Norwegian language, so the commands are English — the radio itself is of course Norwegian.
  3. Try it: say “Alexa, open Norwegian Radio.”

Talking to the skill

The invocation name is “Norwegian Radio”.

Alexa, open Norwegian Radio — starts NRK P1 Stor-Oslo Alexa, ask Norwegian Radio to play P2 Alexa, ask Norwegian Radio to play Klassisk Alexa, ask Norwegian Radio for latest news Alexa, ask Norwegian Radio which channels Alexa, stop · Alexa, pause · Alexa, resume

Use “ask”, not “open”, when naming a channel. “Alexa, open Norwegian Radio to play P2” will not work — open can only launch a skill, it cannot carry an instruction. Ask and tell can.

Norwegian channel names spoken in English are the fiddly part. If a name is not recognised, try the English version — “classical” for Klassisk, “folk music” for Folkemusikk, “always news” for Alltid Nyheter.

About “latest news”

The skill plays the most recent bulletin that has finished airing. At 15:45 that is the 15:00 bulletin; on a weekday at 07:36 it is Dagsnytt 07:30 rather than 07:00, because it follows the actual schedule rather than assuming a fixed hourly rhythm.

It looks at both P1 and P2. This matters more than it sounds: outside summer, P1 broadcasts no bulletins at all between roughly 08:30 and 15:00, filling the window with longer programmes. P2 carries hourly bulletins straight through. Searching both means the newest bulletin is rarely more than an hour old.

If the news sounds older than you expect, it probably is — and correctly so. NRK’s schedule genuinely has gaps. The skill will never invent a bulletin that has not aired, and it will not play one that is still on air.

Troubleshooting

What happens Why, and what to try
Alexa says she doesn’t know that skill The device language must be English (UK) or English (US), and the Echo must be on the same Amazon account the skill was built on.
A channel name isn’t recognised Try the English name, or spell it out (“P one”, “P three musikk”). Not every district name is taught yet.
“Open … to play P2” does nothing useful Use ask instead of open — see the tip above.
News is quieter than the live channels Broadcast recordings are mastered quieter than live streams. The skill prefers the louder source when it can.
Playback stops after a long time Live radio is an endless stream; a network hiccup ends it rather than pausing. Just start it again.

Privacy & data ↑ top

Who is responsible. This skill is an independent, personal project by its developer, who is the data controller for the limited data described here. Contact: sveinungsvea@gmail.com.

What is stored. Nothing. The skill keeps no account, no profile, no history and no settings. It does not use the Reminders, Timers, notifications, location or address APIs, and it asks for no permissions. Each request is answered and forgotten; between requests it holds nothing about you.

What it connects to. To find out what is playing and to build a playable link, the skill contacts NRK’s public programme service (psapi.nrk.no) and NRK’s stream directory (lyd.nrk.no). For recent news bulletins it also calls a small helper page run by the developer on Cloudflare, which returns nothing but a list of NRK addresses. None of these requests carry any information about you — only which channel or programme was asked for.

Who sees what. The audio itself is fetched by your Echo directly from NRK’s servers, not routed through the skill. NRK therefore sees an ordinary listener connection, as it would from any radio app. Amazon processes your speech to run the skill, under Amazon’s own privacy policy. The developer’s helper page and Amazon’s logs may record ordinary technical request logs.

Not shared. Nothing is sold, shared with third parties, or used for advertising or profiling. The skill is not directed to children.

Your control. Because nothing is stored, there is nothing to delete or export. Disabling the skill in the Alexa app stops it entirely. To ask anything about this, contact the developer at the address above.
